Mackenzie (Mac) Lockhart

I am a researcher studying representation, elections, and public opinion. I received my PhD in Political Science from the University of California San Diego in 2023.

I am currently a Democratic Innovations Postdoctoral Associate at Yale's Institution for Social and Policy Studies, where I work with Alan Gerber and Greg Huber on the SAY24 presidential election study.

My research uses surveys and administrative data to analyze voter behavior and legislative representation in American elections. I am particularly interested in how to measure public opinion, how meaningful attitudes are, and what the relationship between policy preferences, candidate positions, and vote choice is.

My work has appeared in the American Political Science Review, American Journal of Political Science, PNAS, and Political Analysis, among others. It has received media coverage in the LA Times, Slate, and Forbes, been cited in amici briefs to the US Supreme Court, and received two APSA section awards.
